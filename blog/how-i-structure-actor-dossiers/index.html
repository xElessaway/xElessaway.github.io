How I Structure Threat Actor Dossiers

A repeatable page model for turning scattered reporting into a dossier that can actually stay current.

April 6, 2026 threat intelligencedocumentationworkflow

Threat intelligence pages become noisy when they try to be complete on day one. In practice, most actor tracking starts thin. The page needs to support uncertainty early and detail later.

That is why every dossier on this site uses the same backbone: summary, aliases, status, targeting, tooling, TTP themes, timeline, references, and related posts. If a section has no value yet, it stays hidden. The template should not force filler copy.

What I optimize for

I care more about update speed than about maximal detail on the first publish. A good dossier should make it easy to add a new campaign, update tooling, or adjust attribution notes without rewriting the entire page.

Why this helps the rest of the site

Once the dossier format is stable, blog posts can feed into it cleanly. A post about a new technique, a campaign note, or an attribution question can later be linked back into the actor page without duplicating the context.
